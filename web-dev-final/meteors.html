Meteor Showers

When the Earth's orbit around the sun takes it through the tail of a comet the night sky becomes streaked with the light of meteors colliding with our atmosphere. It may surprise you that there are many meteor showers throughout the year. Not all of the meteor showers that take place are easily visible though.

Here is some information on the most popular meteor showers that can be seen easily:

Meteor Shower Viewable Associated Object
Quadrantids early-January Minor planet 2003 EH1
Lyrids late-April Comet Thatcher
Perseids mid-August Swift-Tuttle/Comet 109P
Orionids late-October Halley/Comet 1P
Leonids mid-November Tempel-Tuttle/Comet 55P
Geminids mid-December Minor planet 3200 Phaethon
